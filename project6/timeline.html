Visual Timeline of Kewpie Dolls

When one sees a Kewpie, they see the visual figure of cuteness and naivety. Kewpies are child-like, elf-ish, almost angelic beings. Rose O’Neill created the Kewpie character to bring more laughter and fun into the world. After the initial success of O’Neill’s comic strip, the Kewpie character began to be produced as physical dolls and toys for the public. They became to be produced in German factories.

The first Kewpie dolls were produced as bisque dolls, and then they were produced as celluloid dolls. Then later they began to be mass produced as hard plastic dolls. Today, there are various and numerous production material types for Kewpie dolls and the original types are a rare but special find for Kewpie collectors.
